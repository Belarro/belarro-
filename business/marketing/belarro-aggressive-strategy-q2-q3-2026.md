## THE BRAND POSITIONING

### What Belarro Is NOT:
- A microgreens company
- A farming startup
- A supplier

### What Belarro IS:
**Berlin's precision farm for professional kitchens.**

Microgreens today. Gourmet mushrooms next. Whatever the kitchen needs, grown in Berlin, delivered the same day. The product line expands. The brand promise stays the same: **precision ingredients, grown locally, harvested today.**

### The One-Line Positioning:

**DE:** "Belarro — Berlins Praezisionsfarm fuer die Gastronomie."
**EN:** "Belarro — Berlin's precision farm for professional kitchens."

### Why "Precision" Works:
- It's already in your new website ("Precision, Plated.")
- It covers microgreens AND mushrooms AND anything future
- It speaks to chefs — they understand precision. It's their language.
- It differentiates from every competitor who says "fresh" and "local"
- It positions you as a tool for the kitchen, not just a supplier

### The Brand Promise (never changes, even when products change):
1. **Grown in Berlin.** Not imported. Not from Brandenburg. From Prenzlauer Berg.
2. **Same-day harvest.** What we cut today is in your kitchen today.
3. **Precision quality.** Every tray consistent. Every delivery reliable.
4. **Built for chefs.** We exist because professional kitchens need better ingredients.

### Brand Voice:
- Speak like a craftsman, not a marketer
- Concrete, specific, physical language
- "24 varieties" not "wide selection"
- "6 hours from soil to plate" not "ultra-fresh"
- "Soil-grown" not "premium quality"
- Warm but direct. A farmer who respects chefs.
- German for Berlin audiences. English when international context.

### The Three Voices (How Belarro Talks to Chefs)

Every piece of content uses one of three voices. Rotate them for emotional range. Mix: 60% Invitation / 25% Mirror / 15% Evidence.

**Voice 1: THE INVITATION (60% of content)**
Purpose: Build awareness. Show beauty. Educate. Welcome.
Tone: Confident, warm, professional.
The chef feels: Curious. Interested. Wants to know more.

Examples:
- "24 Sorten. Am Morgen geerntet. Mittags bei dir. Aus Prenzlauer Berg."
- "Das ist Red Rambo. Scharf, knackig, und heute Morgen noch in der Erde."
- "So sieht Dienstag bei uns aus: ernten, packen, liefern."

Use for: Variety spotlights, farm BTS, delivery day, brand intros, chef features.

**Voice 2: THE MIRROR (25% of content)**
Purpose: Challenge assumptions. Make chefs question their current supplier. You challenge the SITUATION, not the person. The chef is the hero, not the fool.
Tone: Provocative but respectful. Implied message: "You're too good for what you're using."
The chef feels: Challenged. Thoughtful. Motivated to act.

Examples:
- "Du kontrollierst jede Zutat auf deinem Teller. Aber weisst du, wann deine Microgreens geerntet wurden?"
- "Dein Fleisch kommt vom besten Lieferanten. Dein Fisch auch. Und deine Garnierung?"
- "Du wuerdest nie 3 Tage alten Fisch servieren. Warum 3 Tage alte Garnierung?"

Rules: NEVER mock the chef. NEVER say "you're doing it wrong." Say "you deserve better." The chef is the victim of bad supply, not the fool.

Use for: Wednesday Reels (provocative hooks get reach), LinkedIn thought pieces, Friday posts.

**Voice 3: THE EVIDENCE (15% of content)**
Purpose: Hard comparison with facts. Let the numbers destroy the alternative. No opinions needed.
Tone: Factual. Clean. Almost cold. Present evidence. Step back.
The chef feels: Informed. Rational justification to switch.

Examples:
- "Importiert: 3 Tage alt bei Ankunft. 5 Tage Haltbarkeit. 2.000 km."
- "Belarro: 6 Stunden alt bei Ankunft. 10 Tage Haltbarkeit. 8 km."
- "Der Unterschied ist nicht Meinung. Der Unterschied ist Physik."
- "Gleicher Preis. Doppelte Haltbarkeit. Halb so viel Abfall. Rechne selbst."

Rules: Numbers only. No adjectives. NEVER name competitors — say "importiert" or "Grosshandel." Side-by-side format works best.

## THE TWO-PLATFORM STRATEGY

You have 5 hours/week. Spreading across 5 platforms equally means 1 hour each — too thin to matter anywhere. Instead:

### Primary: Instagram
- Where Berlin chefs scroll daily
- Visual medium — food content performs naturally
- Reels get pushed to non-followers (free reach)
- Direct DM access to every chef in Berlin
- Meta ads cover Instagram + Facebook simultaneously

### Secondary: LinkedIn
- Where restaurant owners and F&B managers make decisions
- Founder story content performs well organically
- Almost zero competition from farms
- 1 post reaches 500+ people without ads

### Parasitic (zero extra time): TikTok, Facebook, YouTube
- Every Instagram Reel gets downloaded and uploaded to TikTok + YouTube Shorts
- Facebook gets auto-posted through Meta Business Suite when you post to Instagram
- Time cost: 10 minutes/week total for all three

## THE 10 RULES

1. **3 Instagram posts/week. Every week. No exceptions.** Consistency beats volume.
2. **Every post starts with a hook.** First line stops the scroll. "Die meisten Microgreens in Berlin sind 3 Tage alt" — not "Hi zusammen."
3. **One CTA per post.** "Schreib uns" OR "Probierpaket" OR "Link in Bio." Never three.
4. **Reply to every comment and DM within 4 hours.**
5. **Never use stock photos.** Only real farm, real greens, real kitchens.
6. **German for Berlin.** LinkedIn can be bilingual. Everything else: German.
7. **Track everything in Google Sheet.** DMs, replies, samples, conversions.
8. **10 outbound DMs/week minimum.** This is where clients come from before the flywheel spins.
9. **Ask every happy client for a referral.** Every. Single. One.
10. **Shoot during activities you already do.** Harvest, pack, deliver — capture what's happening. No extra time.
