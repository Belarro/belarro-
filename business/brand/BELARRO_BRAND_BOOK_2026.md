## 2. BRAND POSITIONING

### The One Line
**"Precision, Plated."**

This is the tagline. It goes everywhere the brand goes. It captures what we do (precision growing) and why it matters (it ends up on a plate).

### What We Are
- A precision farm
- A chef's instrument supplier
- A same-day harvest system
- Berlin-grown, Berlin-delivered

### What We Are NOT
- A microgreens company
- A health food brand
- A sustainability startup
- A lifestyle brand
- A supplier

### The Promise
1. **Soil-grown.** Not hydroponic. Not synthetic sponge. Real soil.
2. **Same-day harvest.** What we cut today is in your kitchen today.
3. **Precision quality.** Every tray consistent. Every delivery reliable.
4. **Built for chefs.** We exist because professional kitchens need better ingredients.

### The Differentiator
Most microgreens in Berlin come from Dutch hydroponic farms — grown on synthetic sponge, fed chemical nutrients, shipped 650km alive in plastic. Chefs cut them themselves.

Belarro grows in soil. Zero additives. Zero kilometers. Harvested this morning. On your station tonight. 10-day shelf life vs 2-3 days.

That's not marketing. That's the product.

## 3. BRAND VOICE

### Tone
- **Direct.** We state facts. We don't persuade.
- **Authoritative.** We know our product better than anyone.
- **Technical.** Precision language. Specific numbers. Named varieties.
- **Restrained.** We let the product speak. No hype, no exclamation marks.
- **Warm underneath.** Not cold or clinical. A craftsman who respects the chef.

### Copy Rules

**DO:**
- Use specific numbers ("24 varieties", "6 hours from soil to plate", "10-day shelf life")
- Use short sentences. One idea per sentence.
- Use concrete nouns and active verbs
- Name the variety ("Red Rambo", not "our radish")
- Lead with the fact, not the feeling
- Use sensory language for products (color, texture, flavor) — this is where precision meets craft

**DON'T:**
- Use exclamation marks. Ever.
- Use "discover", "explore", "journey", "magic", "passion"
- Use "fresh" (too generic — say "harvested today" instead)
- Use "sustainable" unless followed by a specific fact
- Use "we believe" or "our mission" or "our story"
- Use filler words ("really", "very", "amazing", "incredible")
- Use questions as headlines ("Ready to transform your plate?")
- Stack adjectives ("our beautiful, fresh, sustainable, artisan greens")

### Voice Examples

**Good:**
```
"Soil-grown micro-ingredients. Same-day harvest. Berlin."
"We don't hold stock. We grow what your plate is missing."
"Harvested today. On your station tonight."
"25 varieties and growing. Most suppliers offer 5."
"Red Rambo. Deep violet-crimson. Sharp, peppery radish bite."
```

**Bad:**
```
"Discover our amazing range of fresh microgreens!"
"We believe in sustainable farming that makes a difference."
"Join us on our journey to transform Berlin's food scene."
"Our passion for quality shines through in every tray."
"Ready to take your dishes to the next level?"
```

### Bilingual Rules
- **English:** Default for website, international contexts, brand materials
- **German:** Customer communication (WhatsApp, invoices, sales), Berlin-specific marketing
- **Tone stays the same** in both languages. Direct. Specific. No fluff.

**Border System**

| Name | Hex | Usage |
|------|-----|-------|
| **Border** | `#27272A` | Default borders, dividers |
| **Border Light** | `#3F3F46` | Input borders, hover states |

**Accent Colors (Functional Only)**

| Name | Hex | Usage |
|------|-----|-------|
| **WhatsApp Green** | `#25D366` | WhatsApp button only |

**Rules:**
- No brand color. No green-means-farming. No earth tones.
- The palette is achromatic. Photography carries the color.
- White is the only accent. It draws attention through contrast.
- Never add a colored background, gradient, or decorative color.

### Typography

**Typeface:** Inter (Google Fonts)
**One font. All weights. No exceptions.**

| Element | Size | Weight | Case | Letter-Spacing |
|---------|------|--------|------|----------------|
| Hero Title | clamp(3.5rem, 8vw, 7rem) | 700 (Bold) | UPPERCASE | -0.03em |
| Section Heading | clamp(2rem, 4vw, 3.5rem) | 400 (Regular) | Normal | -0.02em |
| Precision Headline | clamp(2.5rem, 5vw, 4.5rem) | 700 (Bold) | UPPERCASE | -0.02em |
| Logo Text | 1.1rem | 700 (Bold) | UPPERCASE | 0.2em |
| Navigation | 0.75rem | 500 (Medium) | UPPERCASE | 0.08em |
| Section Label | 0.75rem | 500 (Medium) | UPPERCASE | 0.15em |
| Product Name | 1rem | 600 (SemiBold) | UPPERCASE | 0.06em |
| Body / Manifesto | clamp(1.2rem, 2.5vw, 2rem) | 300 (Light) | Normal | 0.03em |
| Button / CTA | 0.75-0.8rem | 500-600 | UPPERCASE | 0.12-0.15em |
| Small Text | 0.7rem | 300-400 | Varies | 0.03-0.1em |

**Rules:**
- ALL UI elements (nav, labels, buttons, product names) are UPPERCASE
- Body copy and manifesto text are normal case, light weight
- Headlines are either 700 bold uppercase (impact) or 400 regular (quiet authority)
- Never use italic. Never use underline for emphasis.
- Line height: 1.0 for headlines, 1.6-1.8 for body

### Logo

**Primary Mark:** BELARRO (wordmark, uppercase, Inter 700, letter-spacing 0.2em)

**Usage:**
- Top-left or top-center of any layout
- Always uppercase, always Inter Bold
- Never animated, colored, rotated, or explained
- Sits quietly. Does not demand attention.

**Logo files:** `business/brand/logo/` (PDF, PNG, SVG — two variants)

**Clear Space:**
- Minimum clear space = height of the letter "B" on all sides
- Never crowd the logo with other elements

### Photography

**Product Photography:**
- Dark background, high contrast
- Product as hero — nothing else in frame
- Two modes per product:
  - **Base shot** (luxury/isolated): Product on dark background, clean, minimal
  - **Plated shot** (context): Product on a real plate, in a real kitchen
- File naming: `[product]-luxury.png` and `[product]-plated.jpg`

**Farm/Process Photography:**
- Moody, cinematic lighting
- Show hands, soil, trays, tools — the craft
- Never staged or stock-photo clean. Real work, real light.

**Comparison Photography:**
- Side-by-side format (Belarro vs import)
- Same framing, same lighting for both
- Let the product difference speak

**Rules:**
- No stock photos. Everything real.
- No filters that distort color accuracy (chefs need to see real color)
- No people's faces unless it's a chef feature (the product is the star)
- Dark, high-contrast grading is the default

### Video

**Style:** Cinematic, slow, observational
- Autoplayed, muted, looped
- Dark grading, shallow depth of field
- Types: Hero loop, aerial flyby, process documentation, chef usage
- No text overlays on video. No music. The visual speaks.

## 5. LAYOUT & SPACING

### Grid System
- **Max content width:** 1400px
- **Horizontal padding:** 48px desktop, 32px tablet, 20px mobile, 16px small mobile
- **Section spacing:** clamp(100px, 14vh, 200px) between major sections
- **Card gaps:** 2.5rem desktop, 3rem single-column mobile

### Layout Principles
1. **Full-width sections** for heroes, parallax, forms, final CTAs
2. **Contained content** within 1400px max-width
3. **Aggressive whitespace.** When in doubt, add more space.
4. **Left-aligned text** by default. Center-aligned only for forms and single-line CTAs.
5. **No decorative dividers.** Whitespace is the separator.
6. **No sidebars.** Single-column flow with occasional grids.

### Responsive Breakpoints
| Breakpoint | Target |
|-----------|--------|
| > 1024px | Desktop (full grid, hover states, parallax) |
| 768-1024px | Tablet (2-column grids, reduced spacing) |
| 480-768px | Mobile (single column, hamburger nav, sticky CTA) |
| < 480px | Small mobile (compact type, tight spacing) |

## 6. INTERACTIONS & MOTION

### Animation Principles
- **Entrance only.** Elements fade in and slide up as they enter the viewport.
- **No bounce, no overshoot.** Smooth, controlled motion.
- **Easing:** `cubic-bezier(0.2, 0.8, 0.2, 1)` for exits, `(0.4, 0, 0.2, 1)` for general
- **Duration:** 0.3s for UI responses, 0.8s for content entrances

### Specific Patterns

| Element | Interaction | Behavior |
|---------|-----------|----------|
| Header | Scroll | Transparent → frosted dark glass (blur + opacity) |
| Product Cards | Hover/Tap | Base image fades to plated image (0.8s ease) |
| Precision Blocks | Scroll | Parallax background, content fades in |
| Buttons | Hover | Border + background color invert (white fill, black text) |
| Form Inputs | Focus | Border transitions from dim to white |
| Sticky CTA | Scroll | Slides up on mobile when hero is out of view |
| Page Content | Scroll | Fade-in + slide-up via IntersectionObserver |

### Accessibility
- All animations respect `prefers-reduced-motion: reduce`
- Touch targets minimum 44px on mobile
- Color contrast: White on black = WCAG AAA
- Forms use `aria-label`, `aria-live`, `aria-expanded`

## 8. PRODUCT PRESENTATION

### The Signature Six (Homepage Feature Products)

| Product | Color Description | Texture | Flavor Peak |
|---------|------------------|---------|-------------|
| Red Rambo | Deep violet-crimson | Firm, rigid crunch | Sharp, peppery radish bite |
| Pea Shoots | Vibrant green, cream stem | Juicy, tender, succulent | Sweet, classic & refreshing |
| Sunflower | Vivid green on white stem | Thick, crunchy & substantial | Deeply nutty, raw seeds |
| Broccoli | Delicate meadow green | Soft, fine & feather-light | Mild, clean, highly versatile |
| Nasturtium | Deep rich lilypad green | Smooth, broad surface area | Sharp, wild, intense pepper |
| Amaranth | Vivid magenta-red | Extremely delicate & fine | Earthy, refined & grounded |

### Product Card Format
Every product card has:
1. **Product name** (uppercase, 600 weight)
2. **Three sensory rows:** Color, Texture, Peak (flavor)
3. **Two images:** Base (luxury) swaps to Plated (context) on hover/tap
4. **Mobile hint:** "Tap to see plated"

### Product Description Style
- Lead with color (what the eye sees first)
- Then texture (what the hand/mouth feels)
- Then flavor (the peak experience)
- Each description: 2-4 words. No full sentences. No adjectives that don't add information.
- Every word must be specific. "Peppery" not "spicy". "Magenta-red" not "colorful".

## 9. CONTENT FORMATS

### Social Media (Instagram Primary)

**Visual Style:**
- Same dark aesthetic as website where possible
- Product shots on dark backgrounds
- Farm process in natural light (exception to dark rule)
- Never use templates, stickers, or Instagram native text overlays

**Caption Style:**
- Short. Under 150 words.
- German for Berlin audience.
- One fact per line.
- Hashtags: Max 10, all relevant (#belarro #berlinmicrogreens #microgreens etc.)
- Never: #foodie #yummy #delicious (consumer hashtags)

### Print / PDF Materials
- Same color system (dark background, white text)
- Same typography (Inter)
- Same photography style
- QR code to website (not phone number)
- Minimal information. The PDF is a door, not the room.

### WhatsApp Communication
- Professional but warm
- Short messages (2-3 sentences max)
- Use chef's name always
- Send product photos (from content library)
- Never send voice notes on first contact

## 10. WHAT THIS BRAND IS NOT

These are temptations. Resist them.

- **Not a wellness brand.** We don't talk about superfoods, detox, or health benefits to chefs. Chefs care about flavor, color, and shelf life.
- **Not a sustainability brand.** We are sustainable, but we lead with product quality. Sustainability is a fact in our system, not a selling point.
- **Not a lifestyle brand.** No "farm life" content, no founder story on the homepage, no "our journey" section.
- **Not cheap.** We don't compete on price. We compete on product quality, freshness, and reliability.
- **Not for consumers.** B2B is the business. If B2C comes later, it's a separate brand expression.

## 11. BRAND CHECKLIST

Before publishing anything, check:

- [ ] Is it on dark background? (Or justified exception for farm/process shots)
- [ ] Is the copy specific? (Numbers, names, facts — not adjectives)
- [ ] Is it in Inter typeface?
- [ ] Are UI elements uppercase?
- [ ] Is the CTA one of the approved phrases?
- [ ] Does it avoid forbidden words?
- [ ] Would a chef respect this? (Not a consumer, not a marketer — a chef)
- [ ] Is Belarro positioned as a precision farm, not a microgreens company?
- [ ] Is the German correct? (If customer-facing in Berlin)
- [ ] Does it work on mobile?
