Follow-Up Message Chain — English — Final Version — March 2026
Sales Funnel — First Contact to First Order

Message 1 — First Contact

When: Within 1 hour of leaving the restaurant
Hey [Name], Ron from Belarro. Great meeting you today — hope the samples landed well. Here's everything we grow, with prices: belarro.com/varieties No minimum order. No delivery cost. We deliver every Tuesday, and shelf life is 7–10 days. We grow to order — so everything you get is planted for you. Want me to put you on the schedule for next Tuesday?
→ Send price list image right after this message
CMO: Link moved up for busy chefs. Storyteller: "Planted for you" beats "grow to order to reduce waste." Sales: Ends with yes/no CTA, not "take a look."

Message 2 — First Nudge

When: No reply after 2 days
Hey [Name], curious — did the samples make it onto a plate yet? Would love to know what you thought.
All 3 agents: Kill "just checking in." Storyteller: "Make it onto a plate" speaks chef language. Short, one question, no pressure.

Message 3 — Final Nudge

When: No reply after 5 days
Hey [Name], last thing from me for now — if you ever want to test us, even a small 12 EUR order works. No minimum, no delivery cost. We grow to order so everything arrives at peak freshness. Whenever you're ready, just message me.
STOP. Move to Paused. Re-engage in 2-3 weeks with something new.
CMO: "Last thing from me" creates subtle loss aversion. Sales: Sunday deadline optional add. Storyteller: "We're not going anywhere" energy without saying it.

Message 4 — Chef Replies Positively

When: They say they liked the samples
Really glad to hear that. Just tell me what you'd like and I'll get you set up — we only grow to order, so everything you get was planted for you. Doesn't get fresher than that. If you're not sure what to start with, I can suggest a few things based on your menu.
Storyteller: "Planted for you" is personal. Sales: Last line removes decision paralysis — offer to help choose.

Message 4B — Enthusiastic But No Order NEW

When: Chef says "love it!" or "looks great!" but doesn't actually order
Glad you liked them! Want me to put together a small starter order for next Tuesday? I can suggest what works for most kitchens — or you pick from the list. Even a single item works.
Sales: This is the biggest funnel leak — enthusiasm without conversion. This message bridges the gap.

Message 5 — Order Confirmed

When: Chef tells you what they want
Got it — you're on the schedule. First delivery: [date], around noon. - [items and prices] Total: [X] EUR excl. VAT This repeats every Tuesday automatically. If you ever want to change quantities, swap varieties, or pause — just message me. Simple as that. Looking forward to Tuesday.
→ Confirm delivery address if not already known
Storyteller: "You're on the schedule" makes them feel part of something. CMO: "Simple as that" reinforces ease. Sales: Add invoice mention if relevant.

Message 6 — After First Delivery

When: Wednesday (day after first delivery)
Hey [Name], how did everything look yesterday? Anything you'd tweak for next week — different quantities, different varieties? I'm all ears.
Storyteller: "How did everything look" — chefs assess with eyes first. CMO: Don't repeat "order repeats Tuesday" — they already know. Sales: This opens the upsell window naturally.

Message 6B — After 3rd Delivery — Expansion NEW

When: After 3 consecutive deliveries
Hey [Name], you've had a few deliveries now — how's everything holding up? A lot of kitchens that started with [their items] ended up adding [complementary item] once they saw how guests reacted. Want me to add a small portion to next week's order to try?
Sales: By delivery 3, trust is established. This is the moment to expand the order. Social proof + low-friction upsell.
Re-Engagement

Message 7 — Inactive 2 Weeks

When: Customer paused or stopped for 2 weeks
[Name], everything good on your end? If you want to get your Tuesday delivery going again, just say the word. Same deal — no minimum, no delivery cost.
Storyteller: "Just say the word" is more human than "just let me know." CMO: Dropped "haven't heard from you" — avoids guilt-tripping. Sales: "Was it a menu change?" optional add for intel.

Message 8 — Inactive 1 Month

When: Customer hasn't ordered in 1 month
Hey [Name], been a minute. If your menu's shifted and you need different varieties, happy to work something out. We've also added a few new things lately — worth a look: belarro.com/varieties
STOP unless they reply.
Storyteller: "Been a minute" is natural. CMO: Drop the value prop stack — by message 8 it's wallpaper. Link gives low-effort re-entry point. Sales: Offer in-person sample drop-off if possible.

Message 9 — Re-Engage Paused Lead

When: 2-3 weeks after a lead went silent
Hey [Name], we just started growing [new variety] — [one line about it]. Thought of you. Want me to bring some by this week?
→ Always come back with something NEW. Never "just checking in."
Storyteller: "Thought of you" is the whole message in three words. CMO: Drop the logistics — curiosity alone is stronger here. Sales: "I'm in your area this week" optional add.

Message 10 — Referral (Inbound)

When: A chef recommends another chef
Hey [Name], [Chef] from [Restaurant] thought you'd like what we're growing — fresh microgreens, all grown here in Berlin. Can I drop off some samples this week so you can see for yourself?
Storyteller: "[Chef] thought you'd like" is a recommendation, not a maybe. CMO: "All grown here in Berlin" is a story, not a spec. Sales: "No pitch, just taste" optional add.

Message 11 — Referral Ask NEW

When: Customer has ordered 4+ times and is clearly happy
Hey [Name], really glad the greens are working out for you. Quick ask — if you know any other chefs who'd appreciate what we do, I'd love an intro. Happy to drop off samples to anyone you point me to.
Sales: You have referral inbound but never ASK for referrals. This is free pipeline from happy customers.

Agent Notes — Summary
CMO: Reduce value prop repetition (use in Msg 1 and 3 only). Add social proof where possible. Consider sending a harvest photo before delivery — "your order is growing" — to differentiate from every other text-only supplier.

Sales Head: Every message needs a yes/no CTA. Sunday order deadline creates natural urgency. The "enthusiastic but didn't order" gap was the biggest funnel leak — now covered by Message 4B. Ask for referrals from happy customers.

Storyteller: "Planted for you" > "grow to order to reduce waste." Later messages should get shorter, not longer. Confidence is quiet. Vary the openers — not every message needs "Hey [Name]."
